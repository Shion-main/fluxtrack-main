# Phase 06.1 Plan 04: Heat Grid + Block Saturation Summary

T2's core arithmetic: a campus-wide, Session-based day-by-block utilization grid
that reconciles with T1's headline number, plus the block ranking derived from it.

## What shipped

`scheduling/reporting.py` gains `room_heat_grid(*, start, end, term, as_of=None)`
returning one `HeatRow` per ladder rung, each carrying one `HeatCell` per
`DayOfWeek` in `DayOfWeek.choices` order, and
`block_saturation(*, start, end, term, as_of=None)` returning `BlockLoad` rows
ordered by utilization descending with a block-start ascending tiebreak.

`HeatCell` carries `day`, `used_hours`, `booked_hours`, `available_hours`,
`utilization_pct`, `session_count`, `room_count` and `timetabled`.
`BlockLoad` carries `block`, `used_hours`, `booked_hours`, `available_hours`,
`utilization_pct`, `session_count` and `peak_day`.

Both call `_session_contribution` -- the phase's single definition of "used" --
rather than restating the arithmetic. `room_timetable` was deliberately not
reused, and the docstring says why, so a later reader cannot "simplify" the grid
into a booking count.

## The multi-block distribution rule

A session occupies every rung whose start falls in `[schedule_start, schedule_end)`
(the half-open rule from `web/room_state.py:131`). Its booked and used seconds are
split across those rungs **proportionally to each rung's duration**, integer floor
per part with the remainder handed to the last part, so the parts sum exactly to
the whole. A two-block class therefore contributes its hours ONCE across the grid.

This is the reconciliation property: the grid's summed used hours equal
`room_utilization(...).used_hours` for the same window. Plan 06 renders the T1
card and this grid on one page, and without the property a reader would see two
different numbers with no way to tell which was lying.

## Reconciliation tolerance

Every `Decimal` hours value is quantized to one decimal place, so a single cell
can be off by at most **0.05 h**. The tests assert reconciliation within
`0.05 * cell_count` -- a bound derived from the quantization, not a fudge factor.
Live: grid used 2708.4 vs card used 2707.9 across 77 cells (bound 3.85). Grid
available 10472.1 vs card available 10468.8.

## Deviation from the plan (Rule 2 -- D-10 correctness)

The plan was written against the pre-D-10 model and is wrong in two places:

1. It references `teaching_weekdays`, which D-10 removed. Used `timetabled_cells(term)`
   instead; `{c.day for c in cells}` recovers a day set where one is wanted.
2. Its step 6 specifies a per-cell denominator of
   `physical_rooms x weekday_occurrences[day] x block.duration_seconds` for every
   cell. That is the `days x blocks` cross-product reintroduced one level down: it
   would give Sunday all eleven rungs of capacity on the strength of one class, the
   exact four-point distortion D-10 exists to prevent. **Implemented instead:** a
   cell has capacity only if `(day, block.start)` is in the term's timetabled cell
   set; otherwise `available_hours = 0` and `timetabled = False`.

The `timetabled` flag is a net-new field the plan did not specify. It is required
because "this slot has no capacity" and "this slot had capacity and went unused"
are different facts, and a renderer that shows both as `0%` is lying. Plan 06's
renderer branches on it.

## Live figures (2026-07-13..2026-07-19, as_of today)

- Grid: 11 rows x 7 cells = 77 cells, of which **67 are timetabled** -- matching
  the phase's known cell shape (days 0-5 carry 11 rungs, day 6 carries 1).
- Most saturated: **13:15 at 35%**, 14:30 at 33%, 10:45 at 32%.
- Least saturated: 07:00 at 19%, 18:15 at 16%, **19:30 at 11%**.

That shape is the expected one -- thin at both edges of the day, full through the
middle. A block at 100% would mean virtual rooms leaked in or the denominator
collapsed; none does.

## Tests

`scheduling/tests_reporting_rooms.py`, all shape figures recomputed relationally
from the fixture's own querysets. The reconciliation test is named for the
property it protects and fails if the distribution is changed to replication. The
saturation tiebreak is asserted against a constructed total tie (every session
deleted, so every block sits at 0% and only the tiebreak decides order). The seed
sanity recipe now also prints the grid shape and the saturation head and tail,
with three more falsifiable checks.
