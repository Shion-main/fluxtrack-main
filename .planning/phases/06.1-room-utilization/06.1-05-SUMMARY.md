# Phase 06.1 Plan 05: Per-Room Breakdown + Building/Floor Rollup Summary

The heat grid answers when capacity is idle; this answers where. Together they
turn one campus percentage into a list a facilities officer can walk.

## What shipped

`room_breakdown(*, start, end, term, as_of=None)` returns one `RoomLoad` per
PHYSICAL room, ordered by `utilization_pct` ascending then code ascending -- so
the head of the list is the least-used-rooms answer with no separate query and no
truncation. `building_floor_rollup(...)` reduces that output into a flat
`ScopeLoad` list.

`_range_cell_shape(term, start, end, as_of)` was factored out of
`room_utilization` as the single D-10 denominator walk. Its `cell_seconds` is the
capacity of ONE physical room over the range; campus is that times 125, a floor is
that times its own room count. Every level of the phase's arithmetic is now one
number scaled by a room count, which is what makes the levels reconcile by
construction rather than by luck. `room_utilization` was refactored onto it and
its tests stayed green untouched.

## Rollup structure (plan 06 renders this)

A **flat list** of `ScopeLoad`, not a nested one:

```
level      label               building_code  floor_number  room_count ...
building   Academic Building   ACAD           None          71
floor      ACAD floor 1        ACAD           1             18
floor      ACAD floor 2        ACAD           2             19
building   Admin Building      ADMIN          None          39
...
```

Buildings by code ascending, each immediately followed by its own floors by number
ascending. The template walks one list and applies a single indent class where
`level == "floor"`. Floor rows are keyed on the `(building_code, floor_number)`
pair per `Floor.unique_together`, and each floor label names its building so a
flat table reads without a grouping header.

## Two live findings the plan did not anticipate

**1. Four buildings sit at exactly 0%, and that is correct.** The plan's own
judgement note predicted a 0% building "almost certainly means its rooms are
V-prefixed or its sessions were archived". Neither is true here. MMCM only
timetables classes in ACAD; ADMIN, GYM, IT and UNASSIGNED hold 54 physical rooms
that are offices, a gym and server rooms. They sit in the campus denominator under
D-01, which is precisely why the campus rate reads 26% while the teaching building
reads 46%.

That gap is the honest answer to a question this phase never asked: whether the
denominator should be "physical rooms" or "physical TEACHING rooms". It is a D-01
question and not mine to reopen, so nothing was narrowed. It is recorded in the
seed-sanity recipe as check 8, with an explicit warning not to shrink the
denominator just to make the number nicer.

**2. Some rooms exceed 100%, and that is also correct.** `R116` reads 131%. The
cause is real overlapping data in the imported term: `ECE121L` runs 07:00-10:45 in
that room while `AR111P` (08:15-09:30) and `MMA121` (09:30-10:45) are booked into
it the same morning. The room is genuinely double-booked.

`utilization_pct` is deliberately left unclamped. Clamping would hide exactly the
scheduling conflict a facilities office most wants surfaced, and it is the same
reasoning `room_utilization` already applies to `overrun_sessions`. A room over
100% is information; a SCOPE over 100% would be a bug, since conflicts are local
and a scope averages them away. Both facts are in the `RoomLoad` docstring and in
the recipe as check 9.

## Tests

`RoomBreakdownTests` (9) and `RollupTests` (7). The never-used-room test is named
for the regression it guards -- a future refactor that groups from the session
side. `RoomLoad` is asserted to carry no `seat`/`enrol`/`occupancy_rate` field, so
deferred T3 scope cannot land here without the deferral being reopened. Scope
available hours are recomputed independently in the test as
`room_count x per_room_denominator`. The fixture already contains two buildings
with a floor numbered 1 and a floor numbered 2, so the floor-collision case needed
no fixture change.

Reconciliation across levels is asserted within the quantization bound
(0.05 h per row) where quantized `Decimal` hours are summed, and EXACTLY where raw
seconds are available.
