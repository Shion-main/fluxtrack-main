### Gaps Summary

**One material, empirically-confirmed gap.** The phase's shared merge core, faculty-scan seam,
Checker seam, and sweep-confirmation work (Plans 01-03, and 3 of Plan 04's 4 truths) are all
solidly implemented, wired, and covered by passing tests run live against real MSSQL LocalDB —
ROADMAP criteria #1, #2, #4, and #5 are fully proven. `scheduling/jobs.py` is confirmed
byte-unchanged. No stub code, no debt markers, no orphaned requirements.

However, ROADMAP criterion #3 ("the online analog holds: one Checker verification of a merged
online class covers the co-scheduled online siblings") is **not proven** — it is actively
**disproven** by the phase's own `audit_merge_coverage` tool when run against the real loaded
term, which is precisely the validation step the plan's RESEARCH.md flagged as mandatory before
trusting the "no teams_link clause" design decision (Assumption A1 / Open Q1: "If they share
*only* teams_link, criterion #3 fails for those groups... **[ASSUMED]** — verify against live
term data before coding"). That verification was never actually performed and recorded during
execution — Plan 04's SUMMARY.md describes it only as "Operator follow-up (manual,
non-blocking)" with an expected-but-unconfirmed outcome. Running it now shows: **48 of 152
(~31.6%) real online co-scheduled groups in the active term share neither room nor course_code**
(and, on inspection of a sample, also have a blank `teams_link`), so a Checker's single online
Verify of one sibling will NOT propagate present to the others — those un-verified siblings will
be marked Absent by the sweep once it runs, which is the exact false-Absent failure this whole
phase exists to prevent.

This is not a cosmetic or theoretical issue: it is the phase's stated success criterion, tested
with the phase's own tool, against the phase's own target data, failing at a materially high
rate. It requires either (a) a design fix (e.g., a reliable teams_link-based fallback key, or an
explicit co-schedule marker at import time) plus re-validation, or (b) an explicit, informed
decision from the project owner to accept this gap and amend ROADMAP Success Criterion #3
accordingly — not a silent pass.
