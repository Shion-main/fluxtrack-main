# Phase 04.2 Plan 02: Faculty Scan Seam Summary

**Wired the faculty check-in seam (`web/scan.py:_apply`) to the Plan-01 merge core: both the normal CHECKED_IN and the force-handover ROOM_OCCUPIED outcomes now call `propagate_merged_present` inside the SAME `transaction.atomic()` as the anchor write, so one GARAY scan flips every co-scheduled sibling present (ACTIVE + MERGED + shared actual_start + audited) while the anchor keeps its real method and non-merged rows are untouched — proven by 6 green end-to-end tests.**

## Accomplishments
- **Task 1 — CHECKED_IN propagation:** the CHECKED_IN branch of `_apply` now wraps the anchor `save()` + `audit()` + `propagate_merged_present(session, now, user)` in one `transaction.atomic()`. A single faculty scan on A301 marks A302 ACTIVE + `checkin_method=MERGED` with the same `actual_start` and one `session.merged_present` AuditLog; the anchor keeps `qr_scan`/`manual_code`; the non-merged control stays SCHEDULED.

A repeated merge check-in fills the sibling exactly once (the helper's SCHEDULED status-guard).

## Decisions Made
- **Only present-driving outcomes propagate.** CHECKED_IN and ROOM_OCCUPIED are the two `_apply` paths that flip a session SCHEDULED->ACTIVE, so they are the only two that call `propagate_merged_present`. ABSENT/CHECKED_OUT/EARLY_END/WRONG_ROOM are left unchanged (ABSENT/online propagation is the Plan 03/04 concern via `propagate_merged_absent`).
- **Anchor never re-stamped.** The seam relies on the Plan-01 helper's guarantee that only siblings become MERGED; the anchor keeps its real method in both paths (asserted for `qr_scan` and `force_handover`).
- **Propagate is inside the anchor transaction; notify is outside.** Atomicity (D-04) requires the sibling fill to roll back with the anchor; the force-handover `notify()` is a downstream side effect and stays outside the `with` block.
